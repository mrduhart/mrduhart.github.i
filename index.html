About me

When I was a young child, I kept saying that I wanted to become a scientist and an entrepreneur. Then I grew older and realized that engineering was the way for me to achieve that.

However, I still had to figure out which way to go. I liked a range of topics, from biology, physics and math, to mechanics, electricity and computer science, and there was a major for almost each of these.

This is how I got into robotics. It had the perfect balance of many of the areas that most attracted me. And it was cool.

In that time, I built several projects involving computer vision, mobile robots and hardware & software interfaces. Then, the stuff related to artificial intelligence got my curiosity. I'm talking about building terminators and the Skynet, of course.

So I went into computer science. We were trained in deep learning, traditional and advanced machine learning, and AI. This was when I met Python and fell in love with it. It really helped with my thesis — a data science framework for detecting financial fraud.

School aside, I've also worked as a software developer, building mainly web applications for management, with a bit of the cool things I learned at college.

Now, this unique combination of skills I've acquired have me on the track to fulfilling that childhood dream. I know there are still many things to do to get there, and so I'm constantly learning and trying new stuff.
